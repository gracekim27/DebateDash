Debate Dash

Visualizing debate tournament results and the disparities within them.

Grace Kim and Liam Norman
Why debate?

Debate is more than just an academic competition; it fosters critical thinking, public speaking, and persuasive argumentation. However, disparities in resources, judging biases, and access to opportunities often impact competitive outcomes. By visualizing tournament data, we aim to shed light on these issues and highlight areas for improvement.

What Data?

Our analysis focuses on Lincoln-Douglas Debate results from the 2023-2024 competitive season. We aggregate data from both preliminary and elimination rounds of triple-octa final bid tournaments. These tournaments represent the largest and most competitive events, ensuring consistency in difficulty levels across the dataset.

To analyze disparities, we use the Python library gender-guesser to infer participant genders based on first names. We include only those predictions with high certainty to ensure accuracy. Additionally, we calculate Elo scores for debaters using a base Elo of 200, round multipliers for elimination rounds to account for their increasing importance, and a learning constant of K = 100. The round multipliers are:

  • Prelim: 1.0
  • Quads: 1.1
  • Trips: 1.2
  • Dubs: 1.4
  • Octos: 1.6
  • Quarters: 1.8
  • Semis: 2.0

These adjustments ensure that our analysis accurately reflects the significance of performance in each stage of competition. Let's begin our analysis by looking at overall trends in debate. We can start by looking at an average debater profile.
